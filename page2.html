My Journey and Skills

I’m Jessica K. Baumann, a dual-degree student at Northeastern University majoring in Mathematics and Data Science. Living and studying in Boston has shaped my academic and professional goals, inspiring me to combine technical problem-solving with creativity and collaboration. I’m passionate about using data analysis, mathematical modeling, and visualization to understand complex systems and drive smarter decisions. My coursework and co-op experience have given me practical insight into applying these skills in real-world contexts. At John Hancock, I worked as an Actuarial Co-op, where I automated testing processes and presented analytical findings to leadership. As an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant, I’ve supported hundreds of students learning data science and programming, which has strengthened my technical understanding and leadership abilities. I’m excited to continue developing as a data-driven professional who values accuracy, innovation, and communication. Outside of academics, I enjoy dance, yoga, and exploring Boston’s neighborhoods — activities that help me stay balanced, creative, and inspired both in and out of the classroom.
